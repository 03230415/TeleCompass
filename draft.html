👤 About Us

Meet the Project

🎓
Intern Researcher
Connectivity & Analytics Division

As part of an internship programme, this project investigates the current state of telecommunications and internet connectivity across Bhutan. The work combines survey data, field observations, and interactive visualisations to surface actionable insights for policymakers, service providers, and communities alike.

Guided by the principle of Gross National Happiness, the research aims to bridge the digital divide and ensure equitable access to modern connectivity throughout all 20 dzongkhags — from bustling Thimphu to the most remote highland villages.

20Dzongkhags
500+Respondents
2026Study Year
🏔️

Project Highlights

  • Understanding Mobile & Wi-Fi Usage PatternsMapping how citizens across urban and rural Bhutan access the internet — what devices they use, how frequently, and for what purposes.
  • Analysing Customer SatisfactionMeasuring satisfaction levels with service quality, network reliability, and pricing across Bhutan Telecom, Tashi InfoComm, and Starlink.
  • Creating a Responsive Dashboard for InsightsBuilding an interactive Tableau-powered dashboard that enables stakeholders to explore data visually, filter by region, and spot trends at a glance.
  • Sharing Recommendations for Better ConnectivityTranslating data into practical recommendations — from infrastructure expansion priorities to customer experience improvements and policy proposals.
🧭

Our Mission

To provide Bhutan's telecom ecosystem with a clear, data-driven compass — helping every stakeholder navigate toward a more connected, equitable, and prosperous digital future in harmony with the values of Gross National Happiness.

Key Findings

📱

Mobile Dominance

Over 74% of Bhutanese access the internet primarily via mobile devices, with 4G/LTE being the most used standard in urban centres.

🌄

Rural Gap

Rural broadband access remains at 29%, revealing a significant urban-rural divide that Starlink's satellite service may help bridge in remote dzongkhags.

⭐

Satisfaction Trends

Average satisfaction scores hover around 3.8/5, with network reliability and affordable pricing cited as the top improvement priorities.

🚀

Growth Opportunity

With Bhutan's Digital Drukyul initiative, strong government support exists for expanding fibre and 5G infrastructure, presenting a clear growth runway.

🏢 Telecom Providers

Bhutan's Telecom Landscape

Three key players shaping connectivity across the Kingdom of Bhutan.

📡

Bhutan Telecom Limited

State-Owned · Est. 2000

The national telecommunications company and primary internet service provider in Bhutan. Bhutan Telecom offers fixed-line, mobile (B-Mobile), and broadband services across all 20 dzongkhags, with the widest infrastructure network in the country.

4G LTEFibreFixed-Line
📶

Tashi InfoComm Pvt Ltd

Private · Est. 2008

Bhutan's first and only private telecom operator, operating under the brand TashiCell. Tashi InfoComm has driven competitive pricing and expanded mobile services, particularly in urban areas such as Thimphu and Phuentsholing.

4G LTETashiCellCompetitive Pricing
🛸

Starlink

Global · SpaceX LEO Satellite

SpaceX's low-Earth orbit satellite internet service, increasingly available in Bhutan as an alternative for remote and high-altitude areas. Starlink offers high-speed, low-latency connectivity ideal for Bhutan's mountainous terrain.
